Project Overview

The AI Cultural Journalist is a specialized tool for analyzing African American Vernacular English (AAVE) usage across different cultural domains, news sources, and academic contexts.

Our platform aggregates content from multiple sources, analyzes linguistic patterns, identifies cultural trends, and generates insightful articles that highlight the significance and evolution of AAVE in various contexts.

Key Features

  • AAVE Analysis Dashboard: Comprehensive analysis of AAVE usage patterns across different platforms and contexts.
  • Cultural Insights Dashboard: Visualize content distribution across different cultural domains and sources.
  • Trend Discovery: Identify emerging topics and trends in cultural discourse.
  • Article Generator: Create AI-generated cultural analysis articles based on identified trends.

Cultural Domains

Our analysis focuses on several key cultural domains:

  • Literature & Publishing
  • Academia & Education
  • News Media
  • Entertainment & Arts
  • Social Commentary

By analyzing content across these domains, we can identify patterns in how AAVE is discussed, represented, and used in different contexts.

Research Foundation

This project builds upon pioneering linguistic research on African American Vernacular English, particularly the work "LOW-RESOURCE LANGUAGE MODEL WITH CYBER DEFENSE" (2022).

Our goal is to expand to other languages from the African diaspora globally, including Caribbean Creole languages, West African dialects, Black British linguistic patterns, and Afro-Brazilian Portuguese variations.
